Backrooms (2026) — A Creepypasta Stretched Beyond Its Absolute Limits

By Disillusioned Dev • Published May 2026
3/10
Overall
Score
A24 expands a brilliant 5-minute YouTube video into a sleep-inducing, yellow-tinted commercial waiting room simulation.

Kane Parsons’ original short film was a viral masterpiece of analog horror—liminal spaces, unsettling hums, and pure ambient dread. What A24 and director James Wan failed to realize is that atmospheric dread works best in short bursts.

Stretched to a brutal 104-minute runtime, the feature film strips away the mystery to substitute it with a traditional, agonizingly slow Hollywood narrative. What we get is a group of forgettable researchers wandering down identical fluorescent-lit hallways for an hour and a half.

It doesn’t feel terrifying; it feels like being trapped in a endless, yellow-tinted commercial real estate convention. The original magic is completely diluted.

The Bottom Line

A high-concept internet anomaly dragged kicking and screaming into a standard studio blueprint, losing all its claustrophobic power along the way.
