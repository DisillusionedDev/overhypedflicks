Supergirl: Woman of Tomorrow — Flop of Today

• Published June 28, 2026
2/10
Overall Score
The superhero genre does not demand much in terms of script. Supergirl proves that even this low bar can sometimes be too high

Story & Writing 0.0/10

At the onset itself, Supergirl tempers down expectations on this front by setting up the premise in the most cartoonish way possible. Sadly, what follows isn't any better. The writing formula can easily be summarised as random fights interspersed with either baseless inter-planetary travel or backstory filler with some emotionless exposition sprinkled on top.

The plot revolves around a revenge story. Simple enough, right ? Wrong. What we get is Kara Zor-El asking for directions to find the villains for an unhealthy amount of time. Oh and yes, Lobo exists for some action scenes. Why ? Only God knows.

Directing & Visuals 2/10

Imagine a movie set in a space-western setting. You have effectively endless possibilities in terms of landscaping, colors and the like. It would make sense that Supergirl would capitalize on this, right ? Yes, but if yes meant no.

The design of the sets is bland and monochrome and lacks inspiration. The directing is much worse.

Supergirl frequently alternates between Milly being an absolute powerhouse blasting through tanks in one sequence to being beaten up by random fodder in the next with the whole action sequences being muddied by frequent cuts, leading to an extremely disjointed experience. Neither do we get to see an overpowered Supergirl nor do we see growth-trajectory Supergirl, what we get is just 'Jekyll and Hyde' Supergirl

Acting & Chemistry 4/10

Milly Alcock is the sole saving grace in Supergirl. Whether it's portraying a skeptical, cynical, done-with-the-world girlypop or a girl with a traumatic past laden with grief, Milly Alcock delivers splendidly. It is therefore, even more unfortunate that the script gives her preciously little to work with.

There are seemingly four characters who have the opportunity to gel well together on-screen and establish chemistry. The fact that none of them manage it is a feat in itself. The two main leads - Kara and Ruthye alternate taking up the roles of griever and passive observer during most of their interactions. Lobo is on another planet in terms of the wavelength and dialogue delivery, and not in a good way. Krem attempts to portray himself a sadistic villain throughout the movie without any feats to back it up.

Pacing, Sound & Effects 1/10

I've read positive things about the soundtrack in the movie which forces me to assume that all those people are functionally deaf or their taste in music is absolutely dogshit. The songs and musical cues in the movie feel disjointed and out of place, trying to replicate the delivery of Guardians of the Galaxy without understanding why it worked for them.

The effects do feel nice, I guess. At least we do have some tanks and ships blowing up so the adrenaline junkies can get their rocks off to some extent.

The pacing does serve a purpose - to elongate the movie. Otherwise, the whole movie should rightly have been an anime episode with much time to spare for furthering the story.

The Bottom Line

A movie is allowed to evoke many reactions - Masterpiece, Middling, Migraine-inducing, Manifestation of a Brazen Bull. However, a death sentence of a reaction for a movie is for it to be adjudged as wasting time. Supergirl has evoked this feeling in spades. Watching paint dry, eating glue, bashing my head against a wall are all worthy, productive uses of time as compared to watching Supergirl.
